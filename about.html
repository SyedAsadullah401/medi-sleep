About MediSleep

Sleep disorders disrupt restful sleep, affecting health and daily life. Common types include insomnia (trouble sleeping), sleep apnea (breathing interruptions), restless legs syndrome, narcolepsy (daytime sleepiness), circadian rhythm disorders, and parasomnias (e.g., sleepwalking). Symptoms like fatigue, irritability, and poor focus may stem from stress, medical conditions, or genetics. Treatments include lifestyle changes, therapy, medication, or devices like CPAP. Good sleep hygiene—regular schedules, less screen time, and a calm environment—boosts sleep quality. Untreated, risks include heart disease and diabetes. Consult a specialist for care.
